Q1.1 ordered DELPHI EXPERT CONSULTATION - ROUND 3 Thank you for completing Phase 2 of the Delphi study on evidence-informed and contextually appropriate practical guidance for frontline staff in culturally competent conversations with asylum seekers about their mental health and potential mental health and psychosocial support (MHPSS) needs. We have now reached the third and final round of the exercise. In this round, the statements are preliminarily clustered into 12 domains and are ranked according to the percentage of agreement ('I agree' without modification) across the expert panel based on Round 2. Please confirm your final agreement or disagreement with each of the statements considered for inclusion in the Practical Guide for Frontline Workers in Reception Centres for People Seeking Asylum in High Income Countries. - If you agree that this is an important guideline in this context and it is well formulated, select 'I agree'. - For some statements (depending on the modifications suggested in Round 2): If you agree that this is an important guideline, but you see added value in an alternative wording, select one of the response options starting with 'I agree, but I see added value in formulating it as'. These responses will be collated to the total percentage of agreement for that guideline, the phrase with the most votes is retained. - If you do not agree that this is an important guideline in this context, select 'I disagree'. We end this exercise with a few questions about your expertise. Most of you answered these in the first round, but since the second round we have had the pleasure of welcoming additional experts from whom we do not yet have this information. At the end you can leave your email address if you would like to receive the report of this Delphi study directly in your mailbox. We greatly appreciate the time and expertise that you continue to devote to this exercise, which contributes greatly to moving the asylum reception sector forward in the provision of mental health and psychosocial support. This final evaluation may be a little more time-consuming, but please give a thoughtful response to each statement being considered for inclusion in the guidelines, as you did in the previous two rounds. The value of this Delphi exercise depends on the level of continued participation. The ratings you give to others’ statements will be kept strictly confidential. You can read more about the ethical measures that were taken in this study by clicking here. Before you start, please tick the box to indicate that you agree to take part in this study.
